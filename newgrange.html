Newgrange

Newgrange is a prehistoric monument in County Meath. It was built during the Neolithic Period around 3200 BC. Newgrange is a large mound that measures 76 meters across and 12 meters high and covers 4,500 square meters of ground. It’s circular in shape and made of layers of earth and stone, with a grass growing on top. It’s surrounded with large cobbles.

It is still not clear exactly what it was used for but it is thought to be of religious significance. It’s most famous for the way it is aligned with the rising sun. The sun shines down the chamber on the 21st of december also known as winter solstice, which is the shortest day and longest night of the year. There is only one way in and one way out and this is through a narrow passage. At the end of the passage there are three small chambers of a large chamber with a high roof. Each of these has a large flat “basin stone” Inside the chambers there is a lot of craved artwork in the rocks.

To gain access to Newgrange you must only do so as part of tour. The tour starts at the Bru na Boinne Visitor Center.
